About the Blog Owner

Hey, I’m Brandon! I go by DBSharpy on most places on the internet.1 I’m a Bajan-American game designer, writer and 3D artist from Brooklyn, NY. But…that’s mostly stuff from the portfolio bio. We can be a bit more informal here.

As you could imagine, my interests span much further than the above creative outlets that I want to work in. A brief overview of my biggest hobbies:

  1. As someone who wants to make makes video games, I definitely play and enjoy them. My first love is Yoshi’s Island, but the Ace Attorney series really influenced how I think about video games as a medium. Expect many posts on this subject.

  2. I’ve been dipping in and out of cosplay and prop-making on a regular basis, all starting back in my chuuni phase in high school.2 My old stuff isn’t so impressive that I’ll post any of it, but I’ll likely post new things I attempt.

  1. I played clarinet and piano throughout middle school and high school, giving me a strong appreciation for music. I don’t play either these days, but I’ve been dipping my toes into the stringed instruments pool as of recent.

  2. I really enjoy cooking! Right now, I wanna start dabbling in sauces and drinks in particular, and I’m slowly upping my baking skills. I make a mean macaroni pie though.

  3. I’ve recently gotten into several activities in my endeavors to stay healthy and get fit. Amongst them are running, Dance Dance Revolution, and mixed martial arts! Definitely more of a practitioner than someone who follows any of those things, though.

About the Blog

I made this blog to help encourage me to keep my creative spirit up in my most stagnant times; whether I’m in the midst of unemployment or work I find repetitive, I want to continue to challenge myself, learn new things and, most importantly, express myself. Sometimes this means essays about how a piece of media touched me; sometimes this means photoshopping a statue of a dead philosopher onto a pop star.

Either way, this blog is to help encourage me to both share the things that inspire me and show my process on the things I create.

Feel free to ask me about anything, whether it’s about how I did a thing in 3D or to ask for a recipe. Just keep it cool!
